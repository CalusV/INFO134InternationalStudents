The Academic Quarter

View Description

The Academic Quarter, also called the Quarter, is the student's cultural center in Bergen. The name is a word game, as academic quarter is traditionally the name in which circles formed have put on delays that one should look through the fingers, while a quartet means a quarter. Every year there are a large number of events in the neighborhood, ranging from board games to concerts.

Garage

View Description

Garage is a bar with hangout in Bergen (previously a department also in Oslo, see below) with its own concert department. The Bergens department was founded in 1990 by veterans in the Bergens rock environment, had from the beginning of the rock profile and soon became one of the city's most popular nightlife, especially among students. Due to municipal rules on opening hours, the bar room was divided into two separate departments with different opening hours for several years.

University of Bergen

View Description

With its 16,900 students and 3,700 employees, the University of Bergen is a medium-sized European university. Central parts of the campus are located in the city center. The University of Bergen is both an educational institution and a research institution that covers most disciplines, organized in seven faculties and around 54 institutes and academic centers. The University of Bergen is also part of a global network of students, researchers and knowledge institutions.

Bryggen

View Description

Bryggen in Bergen, also known as the German Bridge and Hansabryggen, encompasses the old wooded and fire-resistant stone jellers in the historic city center of Bergen. Bryggen was built around 1070, and was from 1360 to 1754 the seat of the German office in the city and the central hub of the Hanseatic trade in Norway. The Hansa company was also the Nordic region's first trading company. The bridge consists of approx. 13 goals with 61 protected buildings, [1] and located on UNESCO's list of world heritage sites. Bryggen is the third most visited tourist attraction in Norway.

Old Bergen

View Description

In the 19th century, Bergen was Europe's largest wooden town, a distinctive urban environment with dense wooden houses, busy streets, squares and alleys. At the Old Bergen open-air museum you can relive some of the mood, with houses from the 1700s, 1800s and 1900s. How did it look? What did the people talk about?

Fløyen

View Description

Fløyfjellet or Fløyen is one of Bergen's most popular mountain attractions, and one of Bergen's most popular tourist attractions. The view of Bergen from the viewpoint at Fløyen station Fløyfjellet is first and foremost the name of the mountain top, which is 400 meters above sea level and is located directly above Bergen city center. The name may be from the whistle bar, a windshield or weather rack that was raised to give a signal of the wind direction when ships were to leave Vågen. The view of the mountain peninsula makes Fløyfjellet a popular destination for both tourists and the local people. The Fløyfjellet is also a hiking trail that runs in a loop from Fløien station.
